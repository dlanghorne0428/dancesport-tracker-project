DanceSport Tracker Rating System


About the Elo rating system

  • The Elo rating system was developed to calculate the relative skill levels of chess players.
  • It is also used as a rating system in football, baseball, basketball, pool, table tennis, and various board games and esports.
  • The difference in the ratings between two players serves as a predictor of the outcome of a match. Two players with equal ratings who play against each other are expected to score an equal number of wins.
  • A player's Elo rating is represented by a number which may change depending on the outcome of rated games played. After every game, the winning player takes points from the losing one.
  • The difference between the ratings of the winner and loser determines the total number of points gained or lost after a game. If the higher-rated player wins, then only a few rating points will be taken from the lower-rated player. However, if the lower-rated player scores an upset win, many rating points will be transferred.

Adapting Elo ratings for DanceSport

  • The Elo rating system was designed for two-player games.
  • The DanceSport tracker rating system is based on this article by Danny Cunningham, which defines a multi-player Elo method where each contest is treated a series of head-to-head matchups.
  • For example, a three-couple heat has three distinct matchups (A vs. B, A vs. C, B vs. C)
  • Initial ratings are based on the first event entered by each couple since June 2021.
    • Open Professional events have an initial rating of 1750.
    • Rising Star Professional events have an initial rating of 1500.
    • Amateur Championships and Pro-Am Gold/Open events have an initial rating of 1250.
    • Amateur Pre-Champ and Pro-Am Silver/Closed events have an initial rating of 1000.
    • Pro-Am Bronze/Newcomer events have an initial rating of 750.

Rating Groups

  • Each combination of couple type and dance style (Smooth, Rhythm, Standard, Latin, Cabaret, etc.) have separate ratings.
  • There are five types of couples:
    • Professional Couples
    • Pro-Am Couples - adult student with a pro instructor
    • Amateur Couples - two adult amateurs
    • Junior Pro-Am Couples - junior student with a pro instructor
    • Junior Amateur Couples- two junior amateurs

Rating Points

  • A Couple's rating is based on their performance at NDCA competitions, starting with the Millenium DanceSport in June 2021.
  • All Multi-dance heats count for points.
  • Professional Cabaret / Showdance / Theater Arts events count for points.
  • Single-dance freestyle heats do not count for points. These results are not stored in DanceSport Tracker.
